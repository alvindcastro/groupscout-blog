Building permits were the starting point for Group Scout.

They are a great signal—public, regular, and visible weeks before crews mobilize. But they are not the only way to find high-value hotel leads.

A good signal has four traits:
1. Public and regular.
2. Visible before demand materializes.
3. Correlated with temporary groups.
4. Stable and reliable.

Film and TV productions fit this perfectly. Like construction crews, they have large teams and fixed timelines.

By building a single `Collector` interface in Go, I can plug any data source into the same deduplicate, enrich, and notify pipeline.

Permits show what might get built. Other sources show who is mobilizing.
