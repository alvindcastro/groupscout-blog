Group Scout is more than a permit scraper; it is a signal aggregator.

After construction permits and film crews, I have added a third signal: the sky.

I built `alertd`, a subagent that monitors major airport disruptions—winter storms, equipment failures, and flight cancellation spikes.

Why it matters to a hotel sales manager:
1. Real-time demand. A major disruption means travelers suddenly need lodging.
2. Immediate action. Hotels can adjust pricing, open inventory, and prepare for walk-ins.
3. Rapid response. You have minutes to react, not weeks.

Whether it is a shovel in the ground or a plane on the tarmac, it is all data for the same pipeline.
